South Africa
CAPE TOWN

Cape Town is a port city located on an island beneath the towering Table Mountain on South Africa's southwest coast. Slowly spinning cable cars ascend to the mountain's flat summit, from where breathtaking views of the city, the busy harbor, and vessels bound for Robben Island are available. The infamous jail that previously housed Nelson Mandela and is now a living museum.

Table Mountain

A World Heritage Site for the mountain's unique flora and wildlife are seen nowhere else in the world. There are various hiking paths to explore. While the cable car takes you to the mountain top to view the breathtaking city.

Roben Island

A UNESCO World Heritage Site for being known as the location of a high-security prison where Nelson Mandela and other political prisoners were imprisoned under the apartheid regime.

Cape Point

Cape Point Nature Reserve is a gorgeous location with lush hills, a rocky shoreline, and breathtaking scenery. Baboons, Cape fur seals, and birds are among the animals that call the area home.

Groot Constantia

A popular tourist destination in Cape Town's Southern Suburbs. It is South Africa's oldest wine estate and produces some of the best wines in the country.

V&A Waterfront

The Victoria & Albert Waterfront in Cape Town is one of South Africa's most famous tourist sites. It is located on the Atlantic Ocean and provides incredible views and a selection of stores and restaurants.

Kirstenbosch Botanical Garden

Located at the base of Table Mountain, is considered one of the world's most beautiful gardens occuping over 528 hectares and home to an astounding variety of plant life.

Bo Kaap

The Bo-Kaap is one of Cape Town's most unique neighbourhoods, with plenty to see, taste, and discover.

Blaauwberg

Blaauwberg, or "Blouberg," as it is commonly known by locals, is an exquisite length of coastline located just a short drive from Cape Town's city centre.
