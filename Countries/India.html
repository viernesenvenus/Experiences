India
New Delhi

Laid out by British architect Edwin Lutyens, the Indian capital is a striking modern metropolis. A symbol of the country's rich past and thriving present, Delhi is a city where ancient and modern blend seamlessly together. A gracious contrast to Old Delhi's winding streets, the grand avenues and stately buildings of New Delhi are rich with history and culture, from Gandhi's Delhi home (and the site of his assassination) to the tomb of Humayun, a complex of Mughal buildings reminiscent of the Taj Mahal.

India Gate

At the centre of New Delhi stands the 42 m high India Gate, an "Arc-de-Triomphe" like archway in the middle of a crossroad. It commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during the World War I and bears the names of more than 13,516 soldiers killed in the Northwestern Frontier in the Afghan war of 1919.

Red Fort

The Red Fort or Lal Qila is a historic fort in Delhi in India that served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. Visit red fort to experience the rich history and cultre of india and shop in the meena bazaar hosted in the courtyard just like the old times.

Humayun's Tomb

Located in New Delhi’s Nizamuddin East neighbourhood, A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Humayun’s Tomb lies on the banks of the Yamuna, in proximity to the dargah of popular Sufi saint Nizamuddin Auliya.This tomb, built in 1570, is of particular cultural significance as it was the first garden-tomb on the Indian subcontinent.

Qutub Minar

Qutab Minar is a soaring, 73 m-high tower of victory, built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak immediately after the defeat of Delhi's last Hindu kingdom.It is said that if you can encircle it with your hands while standing with your back to it your wish will be fulfilled.

Akshardham Temple

Swaminarayan Akshardham in New Delhi epitomizes 10,000 years of Indian culture in all its breathtaking grandeur, beauty, wisdom and bliss. It brilliantly showcases the essence of India’s ancient architecture, traditions and timeless spiritual messages. The Akshardham experience is an enlightening journey through India’s glorious art, values and contributions for the progress, happiness and harmony of mankind.

Lodi Garden

Lodi Gardens is a city park situated in New Delhi, India. Spread over 90 acres, it contains Mohammed Shah's Tomb, the Tomb of Sikandar Lodi, the Shisha Gumbad and the Bara Gumbad. An oasis in an otherwise crowded, dusty city, Lodi Gardens is the perfect place to spend a relaxing afternoon admiring nature.

Lotus Temple

Lotus temple is built in the shape of a lotus flower and is the last of seven Major Bahai's temples built around the world. Completed in 1986 it is set among the lush green landscaped gardens.This architectural wonder looks like an enormous lotus blossom and is reminiscent of the Sydney Opera House.

Gurudwara Bangla Sahib

Gurudwara Bangla Sahib is a Sikh temple as well as a tourist place in Delhi. A beautiful Sikh temple with golden onion domes and a sacred lake, the rhythmic chanting of the Sikhs makes it a truly spiritual place to wander. If you wish to explore art, culture, religion and history of Delhi, then Gurudwara Bangla Sahib should be on your list of places to visit in Delhi.
